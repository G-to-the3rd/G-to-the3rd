Introduction

Computers, in modern standards, are highly complex devices that quickly process data. Of course, computers would not be what they are today without the milestones and achievements beforehand. Here are some of the most notable historical computers that have made significant impact on technological development.

The Human Hand

Before 2000 B.C., human fingers were considered the earliest computing devices. It allowed counting in multiples of 5 due to having 5 fingers each hand.

The Abacus

Due to the limitations of counting with the human hand, the Middle East began counting through the use of stones in trays. The Babylonians later adapted this idea into the development of the Abacus.

The Pascaline

A computing device invented by Blaise Pascal in 1791.

The Difference and Analytical Engine

Two devices invented by an Englishman mathematician and inventor, Charles Babbage. The difference engine solves polynomial equations through the methods of differences. While the analytical engine is designed as a general computing device.

The Tabulating Machine

A device consisting of punch cards and a punch card reader, thus allowing it to "tabulate" data. Invented by Herman Hollerith in 1880.

Mark I

An electromechanical device built for the navy. It primarily utilizes relays.

Colossus

A device built for the British. Utilized for wartime code-breaking of German radio transmission

Atanasoff Berry Computer or ABC

The first ever electronic digital computer. It was constructed at IOWA state.

ENIAC

The most famous of the early computers containing roughly 18,000 vacuum tubes, used by the army for ballistic calculations.

EDVAC

The first ever stored memory computing device.

UNIVAC

The first ever commercially available computer, primarily used for businesses.
